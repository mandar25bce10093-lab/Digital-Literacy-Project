Case Study: 
The "Request Money" UPI Scam
What it is: UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of financial cybercrime where scammers trick victims into authorizing unauthorized debit transactions. Rather than hacking the system's infrastructure, criminals exploit human psychology and a misunderstanding of how digital "collect" requests work.
How it Typically Happens (Step-by-Step):
1.	The Hook: A scammer poses as a buyer on a platform like OLX or a customer service agent on social media. They express interest in an item the victim is selling or offer a "refund" for a failed transaction.
2.	The Deception: The scammer tells the victim they will pay via UPI. Instead of sending money, they use the "Request Money" feature or send a QR code via a messaging app.
3.	The Misdirection: The criminal insists that the victim must "scan the code and enter their UPI PIN" to receive the funds. They often use high-pressure tactics, claiming the link will expire in minutes.
4.	The Transaction: Trusting the caller, the victim enters their PIN. This action technically authorizes a debit from their account.
5.	The Theft: Funds are instantly transferred to a "mule" account. Once the money is moved, the scammer immediately blocks the victim.
Who is Targeted: Anyone using digital payment apps is a potential target, but university students selling used items and individuals seeking technical support online are particularly vulnerable due to frequent digital interactions.
Consequences: The immediate consequence is the loss of funds, which is difficult to reverse because UPI transactions are instantaneous. Beyond financial loss, victims often experience a significant loss of trust in digital systems and psychological stress from the targeted manipulation.
