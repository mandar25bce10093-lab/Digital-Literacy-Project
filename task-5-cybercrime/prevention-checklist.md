Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for Students
Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Use app-based authenticators (like Google Authenticator) rather than SMS-based 2FA for your email, GitHub, and social media accounts to prevent unauthorized access.

Verify UPI "Collect" Requests: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. A PIN is only required to send money or check your balance. If someone asks for a PIN to "verify" a payment to you, it is a scam.

Audit App Permissions: Regularly check which apps have access to your contacts, location, and microphone. Delete "cleaner" or "battery saver" apps that often act as spyware on older devices.

Secure Your Hardware: Use a strong alphanumeric password for your laptop and mobile devices. If your battery is depleting rapidly, ensure it’s not due to background malicious processes by checking your resource monitor.

Use Official UPI Apps Only: Only use verified UPI applications (like BHIM, Google Pay, or PhonePe) and never click on third-party links sent via WhatsApp or SMS to "update KYC."

Practice "Clean" Social Media: Avoid using "Mid" culture puns or trendy bios that reveal your specific location, full name, or birthdate, as these can be used for identity theft or targeted phishing.

Public Wi-Fi Hygiene: Avoid logging into bank accounts or student portals over public campus or cafe Wi-Fi. If necessary, use a trusted VPN to encrypt your connection.

Report Immediately: If you notice an unauthorized transaction or cyber harassment, act within the "Golden Hour" (first 2 hours) to increase the chances of fund recovery.
